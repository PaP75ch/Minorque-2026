Late July gets hot

The week’s rhythm

We shape the day around the heat and the little ones’ naps: the best happens early morning and in the evening, with real downtime in mid-afternoon.

☀️ 8h – 13h

Morning, the action

Beaches and calas early (before the crowds and full car parks), village strolls in the shade. This is outing time.

🌿 13h – 17h

Afternoon, the calm

Back to the villa, lunch, Apolline’s nap, private pool and shade. We don’t fight the 3pm sun — we use it to breathe by the pool.

🔥 19h – tard

Evening, together

Barbecue at the villa or a restaurant. Dinners lean fish & chicken, with vetted, well-rated spots.

By theme · supplies

Groceries & supermarkets

Quick top-ups are around the corner; for the big weekly shop (10 people — it adds up!), Maó’s big stores are cheaper. Distances from the villa.

💡
The smart supply plan

Do the big shop on Maó day (D6): combine the visit with a full Mercadona / Lidl run, far cheaper than Son Bou’s minimarkets. The corner shop (Sant Jaume, 4 min walk) stays perfect for bread, aperitivo and forgotten items.

We’ve got a barbecue

Barbecue ideas · fish & chicken

Easy villa evenings with the kids playing nearby. Top up at Maó market for fresh fish and the Alaior cooperative for meat.

🍗 Chicken
  • Lemon · oregano · garlic skewers
  • Thighs in honey & smoked paprika
  • Rosemary drumsticks for the kids
🐟 Fish
  • Whole sea bream or bass, rock salt & fennel
  • Garlic prawn skewers
  • Tuna / swordfish steaks from the market
🥗 On the side
  • Grilled vegetables (peppers, courgettes)
  • Foil-baked potatoes, allioli
  • Local aperitivo: Mahón cheese, sobrassada
